History [ edit ] 
It was founded in 1799 by Armenian settlers from Derbent. [ 7 ] During World War II, Budyonnovsk was occupied by German troops from August18, 1942 to January10, 1943.
The Budyonnovsk hospital hostage crisis took place here on June 1995.
Administrative and municipal status [ edit ] 
Within the framework of administrative divisions, Budyonnovsk serves as the administrative center of Budyonnovsky District, even though it is not a part of it. [ 1 ] As an administrative division, it is incorporated separately as the town of krai significance of Budyonnovsk aan administrative unit with the status equal to that of the districts. [ 1 ] As a municipal division, the town of krai significance of Budyonnovsk is incorporated within Budyonnovsky Municipal District as Budyonnovsk Urban Settlement . [ 2 ] 
Military [ edit ] 
An airbase is located 9 miles (14km) northwest of the town.
Religion [ edit ] 
On July1, 2010, an Armenian apostolic church was opened in Budyonnovsk. [ 11 ]
